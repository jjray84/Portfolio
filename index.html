Wander Away

About Me

All we have to decide is what to do with the time that is given to us. If all of us valued food and cheer and song above hoarded gold, it would be a merrier world. But in the end it's only a passing thing, this shadow; even darkness must pass. It's dangerous business going out your door. You step onto the road, and if you don't keep your feet, there's no knowing where you might be swept off to. The world is indeed full of peril, and in it there are many dark places; but still there is much that is fair, and though, in all lands, love is now mingled with grief, it grows perhaps the greater. It's not the strength of the body, but the strength of the spirit. Folk seem to have been just landed in adventures, usually - their paths were laid that way. But I expect they had lots of chances, like us, of turning back, only they didn't. And if they had, we shouldn't know, because they'd have been forgotten. Not all those who wander are lost.
